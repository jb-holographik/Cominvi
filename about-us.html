About us

A company built on history, driven by values, and committed to doing mining differently.

From our roots in Guanajuato in 1997 to today’s internationally certified operations, CoMinVi blends experience, ethics, and vision to transform underground mining in Mexico.

S.02
Our History
From 1934
The storyof aFamily
To 2025
1934

The story of CoMinVi begins in 1934, when Don Rafael Villagómez Hernández joined the “El Cubo” mine in Guanajuato.

1965

His son, Rafael Villagómez Mapes, followed in his footsteps in 1965 and later became an independent mining contractor, laying the groundwork for a family legacy in underground mining.

1997

In 1997, Lic. Rafael Villagómez Contreras gave new life to that legacy. With hands-on experience and a clear vision, he began taking on quarry work—starting small but driven by a deep commitment to quality. As projects grew, so did his team and equipment.

2002

In 2002, CoMinVi was officially established in the state of Guanajuato. Since then, we’ve grown project by project, staying true to a story built on real work, clear vision, and pride in where we come from.

Mission

Ethics

We are a construction company specialized in mining infrastructure, dedicated to delivering safe, efficient, and sustainable solutions. Our commitment is rooted in operational excellence, technological innovation, and social responsibility—driving the growth of the mining industry while improving the well-being of the communities where we operate.

To be recognized as global leaders in mining infrastructure construction, setting new standards in safety, efficiency, and sustainability. We aim to transform the industry through constant innovation and contribute to the economic and social development of the regions where we operate—while preserving the environment for future generations.

At CoMinVi, we believe ethical conduct is the foundation of a trustworthy and sustainable company. Our business ethics code aligns with the principles of the United Nations Global Compact and the Mexican Constitution. We actively foster a culture of integrity across all levels of the organization—from partners and managers to suppliers. We uphold the UN’s 10th principle against corruption and enforce strict policies on anti-corruption, human rights, and professional conduct. Our goal is not just to comply with the law—but to go beyond it and set an example of honest, responsible conduct in the Mexican mining industry.

S.05
Environment

At CoMinVi, responsible mining begins with respect for the environment. Our environmental strategy is built on prevention, regulatory compliance, and continuous improvement through the SISSMAC system.

Though we operate underground, we implement strict controls to minimize emissions, manage waste responsibly, and protect air quality at all our sites.Properly executed underground mining already reduces surface impact.

We take it further, optimizing resources, minimizing waste, and building a strong environmental culture within our teams. Our commitment is clear: to protect the environment with the same seriousness with which we protect our people.

S.06
Social Impact

We believe true excellence is earned and certified. Our commitment to safety, quality, social responsibility, and ethics is backed by internationally recognized standards and institutions.

Our Certifications
01

Socially Responsible Company (ESR)

For the tenth consecutive year, the Mexican Center for Philanthropy (CEMEFI) awarded CoMinVi the distinction of Socially Responsible Company (ESR). This is both a great honor and a significant responsibility.

02

ISO 45001:2018 &
ISO 9001:2015

At CoMinVi, safety and quality go hand in hand. We certify our Safe Work Procedures (PST) and Quality Management Administrative Processes in full compliance with international standards:
- ISO 45001:2018 Occupational Health and Safety Management System 
- ISO 9001:2015 Quality Management System

03

United Nations Global Compact

In line with the 10 Principles of Corporate Social Responsibility, we joined the United Nations Global Compact three years ago to strengthen human rights, labor standards, environmental protection, and anti-corruption efforts.

04

Risk & Process Mapping

Tailored risk management adapted to organizational needs. In Mexico, compliance is regulated by the Secretariat of Labor and Social Welfare (STPS), while internationally, ISO 45001:2018 provides a recognized global framework for occupational health and safety. Adopting these standards not only reduces incidents but also boosts team morale and strengthens organizational reputation.
